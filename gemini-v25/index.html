The Book of Spirits – Prolegomena

For those who believe they are seeing the dawn of a new era, it is necessary to understand the true nature of the spiritual world...

Chapter 1 – God and Creation

God is the supreme intelligence, the primary cause of all things. He is eternal, immutable, immaterial, unique, all-powerful, sovereignly just and good.

The universe is a manifestation of His power and wisdom. Spirits are His creations, endowed with free will, progressing through different degrees of purification across multiple existences.

Chapter 2 – The Spirit World

Spirits are not abstract beings. They are real, individual entities that populate the invisible world. They can communicate with the living through mediums, and their influence is constant in human affairs.

There are spirits of all degrees: from the most ignorant and perverse to the most elevated and pure. The latter are the guardians and protectors of humanity.

Chapter 3 – Reincarnation and Progress

Reincarnation is the law of progress. The soul passes through multiple corporeal existences, each one offering new opportunities for moral and intellectual advancement. Suffering is the result of past errors and serves as a means of expiation and improvement.

The destiny of man is in his own hands. Through his actions, thoughts, and feelings, he builds his future happiness or unhappiness.

Chapter 4 – Moral Laws

The highest law is that of love and charity. "Do unto others as you would have them do unto you." This is the foundation of all true progress, both individual and collective.

Material possessions are temporary. Only moral qualities survive physical death and accompany the spirit into the next life.

Chapter 5 – Prayer and Meditation

Prayer is a powerful force that elevates the soul. It is not a request for material favors, but a means of spiritual communion with higher intelligences and with God.

Meditation allows man to reflect on his actions and align himself with divine laws. Regular practice leads to inner peace and moral elevation.
